Summary

I'm an ongoing Controls Systems intern at Barnes Aerospace, supporting the company's Smart Connected Factory initiative: the broader effort to instrument and modernize plant operations through SCADA, PLCs, and connected sensor systems. My work sits on the controls and automation side of manufacturing.

Most of what I do supports two areas: building SCADA dashboards that give plant teams better real-time visibility into process and equipment status, and helping scope and prepare sensor integrations to bring additional process parameters under continuous monitoring. Across both, I work cross-functionally with maintenance, environmental and safety, and operational teams.

Note: Specifics on individual projects, facilities, and equipment are omitted from this page pending review of what's appropriate to share publicly. I'm happy to discuss the work in detail in conversation, please feel free to reach out.

What I've Worked On

  • SCADA dashboard development in Ignition Perspective. Designed and built operator-facing interfaces that surface real-time equipment and process status, historical trending, and alert states. Heavy emphasis on visual clarity so plant staff and maintenance can read system state at a glance.
  • Sensor selection and integration planning. Researched, evaluated, and recommended sensor options for new monitoring loops, weighing signal integrity, environmental durability, and integration cost. Worked with vendors and internal stakeholders to scope what's required to bring new measurements into the existing control network.
  • Environmental and safety monitoring support. Contributed to projects aimed at improving real-time visibility into safety equipment readiness and at automating environmental compliance measurements that had previously been manual.
  • Cross-functional planning and risk assessment. Participated in planning conversations across maintenance, environmental and safety, and operational teams. Helped scope installations to minimize downtime and align with regulatory requirements.

What I've Taken Away

  • How a real industrial controls environment fits together: SCADA, PLCs, sensors, and the people who keep them running. Software, hardware, and operational reality are equally important, and weakness in any one of them shows up downstream.
  • That dashboard design is a real engineering skill. A clear, well-organized SCADA screen genuinely makes plant operations safer and faster, and a cluttered one is actively dangerous. The visual side of controls work matters more than I'd expected coming in.
  • How to operate cross-functionally. Most of my projects touch maintenance, environmental and safety, and operational teams simultaneously, each with different priorities and constraints. Learning to translate between them is a core part of the job.
  • The value of methodical problem-solving when there isn't an obvious answer. Most of what I work on doesn't have a textbook solution and it's a matter of researching options, weighing trade-offs, and making a defensible recommendation that other people can act on.
